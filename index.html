// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your project images are functioning as decorative files with zero structural data attached — no JSON-LD schema marking up your architectural projects, no descriptive alt-text on portfolio shots, and no EXIF location data tying images to place. A high-intent query like "residential architect Golden Gate Heights San Francisco" is bleeding to competitors right now because your featured Dwell project exists as a picture on a page, not as a structured entity search engines can parse and serve. You have a Forbes Top 200 credential and a Dwell feature — both are citation-grade trust signals — and neither is wrapped in schema connecting it to a local or project-type search result. That's not a future opportunity you're missing. That's traffic you're forfeiting today, on the exact searches your ideal clients are running.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: A homeowner or developer evaluating a $500K–$2M+ commission doesn't convert on language alone. "Distilled, open, and enduring architecture with strong character" is a claim with zero visual proof behind it — no process documentation, no annotated before/after sequences, no material detail photography that validates the craft. Your Dwell Golden Gate Heights before/after is the closest thing to conversion evidence on the site, and it lives inside someone else's editorial framing, not yours. To a sophisticated buyer, that gap doesn't read as "small firm." It reads as "the work is real but unproven on their own terms" — and that's the exact hesitation that kills a commission this size.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Firms operating at your tier — SHED Architecture, Feldman Architecture — are running full project documentation sets: construction-phase photography, material close-ups, occupancy-phase lifestyle sequences, drone site-context shots establishing neighborhood anchoring. You have Forbes Top 200 recognition and a Dwell feature with no visible asset class showing how a project is actually built and inhabited over time. That absence hands your referral-stage decisions — where a past client recommends you to a peer — to firms whose websites close the argument for them before you're even in the conversation. You're not losing those commissions because the work is weaker. You're losing them because the documentation is structurally thinner than the work deserves.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Treat your next project shoot as a structured data deployment, not a portfolio refresh. That means four asset layers: site-context establishing shots with geotagged EXIF, material/detail photography with descriptive filenames tied to spec language, occupancy sequences that visualize the "sense of connection and belonging" you already claim, and a compressed before/after set you own outright — independent of Dwell's editorial control. Every image ships with a mandatory metadata protocol: descriptive alt-text, structured captions referencing project location and typology, and JSON-LD markup linking each image to a project page with schema-coded architect, addressRegion, and award fields. This converts your photography from a static archive into a search-indexed, trust-building, referral-closing asset — one that does the work of selling the next commission before you ever pick up the phone.
